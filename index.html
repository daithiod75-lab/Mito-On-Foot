10:10 AM - 10:55 AM 45 Mins

Kodokan Han School

~600m (Walk: ~8-10 mins)

The largest samurai academy of the Edo period, founded in 1841. Students mastered medicine, math, martial arts, and Confucian philosophy.

India Connection:

Mirrors ancient Indian higher learning academies like Nalanda or Takshashila Gurukuls, combining martial and deep academic teachings under disciplined lifestyle codes.

💡 Youth Tip:

Awesome backdrop for photos! Spot the old gate bullet holes from the 1868 Battle of Mito, showing real samurai military drama.

11:15 AM - 12:45 PM 90 Mins (Lunch)

Mito Keisei Department Store

~1.5 km (Walk: ~20 mins)

Mito's modern shopping landmark. Cool window-shopping, cosmetics, and a wonderful top dining floor perfect for lunch breaks.

India Connection:

Relates directly to India's high-end lifestyle complexes like Select Citywalk in Delhi or Phoenix Palladium in Mumbai. The basement "depachika" food hall mirrors boutique sweet bazars!

1:05 PM - 1:55 PM 50 Mins

Kairakuen Garden

~1.5 km (Walk: ~20 mins)

One of Japan's Three Great Gardens, constructed in 1841. Famous for 3,000 plum trees and deep bamboo groves.

India Connection:

Compares with imperial botanical estates in India, like Srinagar's Shalimar Bagh or Mysore's Brindavan Gardens, honoring symmetry, nature, and flow.

2:10 PM - 2:35 PM 25 Mins

Lake Senba & Park

~1.2 km (Walk: ~15 mins)

A beautiful calabash-shaped lake full of swans and ducks. Highly popular for student leisure.

India Connection:

Resembles popular urban leisure lakes like Sukhna Lake in Chandigarh or Kankaria Lake in Ahmedabad, busy with pedal boats, cyclists, and families.

2:58 PM Arrived

Return: Mito Station

~1.8 km (Walk: ~22 mins)

Arrive safely at Mito Station before 3:00 PM to maximize afternoon shopping window.

India Connection:

Before leaving, try Mito's famous Natto (fermented soybeans). Its distinct preparation mirrors northeast Indian regional delicacies like Akhuni of Nagaland or Hawaijar of Manipur.

Delicious vegetarian-friendly lunch options located within walking distance of Keisei Department Store.

Vegetarian-Friendly Curries

Taj Mahal Mito (インド料理 タージマハル)

Highly reliable Indian kitchen with English speaking staff. Great Chana Masala, Dal, and freshly baked naans.

Premium Coffee & Sweet Potato Desserts

Saza Coffee (サザコーヒー 水戸京成店)

A legendary coffee chain from Ibaraki. Excellent sweet potato options, castella cake, and matcha beverages.

Organic & Plant-Based Platter

Organic Cafe Yosou (オーガニックカフェ ヨソウ)

Uses pesticide-free farm-grown vegetables to construct healthy, visually stunning macrobiotic lunch plates.
